Taiwanese cuisine

Taiwanese cuisine is known for its distinctive flavors, and two dishes stand out: stinky tofu and hotpot. Stinky tofu, despite its strong smell, is a popular street food that surprises and delights the senses. Its crispy exterior hides a flavorful interior, and when paired with tangy sauce and garnishes, it creates a unique eating experience. Hotpot, on the other hand, brings people together around a bubbling pot of broth filled with fresh ingredients. The communal cooking and dipping in savory sauces create a symphony of flavors that showcase Taiwan's vibrant food culture. These traditional dishes truly capture the essence of Taiwanese cuisine.
